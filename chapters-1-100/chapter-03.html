Chapter 3: Cut shot system from loose without spin (part 02)

3.1 Introduction

In this chapter we cover the Cut Shot System ‚Äúfrom loose without spin‚Äù. This system is applied when the cue ball must strike a thin cut without using spin, whereby the natural running line of the ball determines the trajectory.

This system is especially useful in situations where spin is undesirable, for example:

  • short distances between cue ball and object ball
  • sensitive positions where spin causes too much deviation
  • tables with strongly dragging or very sliding cloth

The goal of this chapter is to give the player insight into the natural lines of the cue ball and how to use these optimally without spin.

3.2 Basic principle of the Cut Shot System without spin

When the cue ball is played without spin, it follows a predictable and relatively stable line. The change of angle after contact with the object ball depends on:

  • the thickness of the cut
  • the speed of the cue ball
  • the distance to the first rail
  • the position of the second ball

The basic rule for this system reads:

‚û§ The thinner the cut, the straighter the line towards the first rail.

This means the player must pay particular attention to the exact thickness of the object ball contact to create the correct running line.

3.3 Reference points and counts

In contrast to systems with spin, the reference points are simpler here. The player focuses mainly on:

  • the natural angle of the cue ball
  • the position of the second ball
  • the distance to the first rail
