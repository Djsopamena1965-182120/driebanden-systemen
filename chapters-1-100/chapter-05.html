5.1 Introduction

The Cut Shot System LSL (Long‚ÄìShort‚ÄìLong) is one of the most widely used patterns in three‚Äëcushion billiards. This system is applied when the cue ball departs via the long rail, then hits the short rail, and afterwards the long rail again to reach the third cushion.

This system is particularly useful when the cue ball must strike a thin cut shot and the natural line is insufficient to create the correct angle. The player must find a balance between thickness, spin, and speed.

5.2 Basic Principle of the LSL System

The basis of the LSL system is the relationship between:

  • the thickness of the object ball
  • the amount of spin
  • the distance to the first rail
  • the desired arrival line on the third rail

The player must pay close attention to the balance between spin and thickness. A ball struck too thick gives too little angle, while a ball struck too thin gives too much angle.

5.3 Reference Points and Corrections

The reference points for the LSL system are based on fixed values on the long and short rails. The player first determines the departure point on the long rail, then the desired arrival point on the third rail.

Corrections are needed when:

  • using 2 or 3 spin
  • long distance to the first rail
  • high speed
  • tables with a lot or little grip

The more spin, the longer the cue ball runs after the rail.
